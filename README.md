The housing market is one of the most active and important industries in the economy, where house prices change as a result of many determinants. It is a difficult but crucial task to forecast house prices accurately for homebuyers, home sellers, investors, and policy makers. It is possible to make informed decision-making and investment planning based on the interaction of different house characteristics and their prices in the market.

Historically, house price approximation was based chiefly on professional assessments, past performances analysis, and manual comparisons. These traditional means, though, tend to bog down when processing the increasing depth and volume of contemporary real estate information. Such is where  Machine Learning (ML)  prevails by assessing large data collections, discovering elusive patterns, and providing precise forecasting.

The overall objectives of this project are:
•	To determine how various factors affect house prices.
•	To use machine learning models (such as Linear Regression, Decision Trees, and Random Forest) to make precise price predictions.
•	To compare and assess the performance of these models using the right metrics.
•	To graph trends and relationships in the dataset for easier interpretation.

Through the successful deployment of this system, the project illustrates how machine learning can be used to solve real-world issues, providing quicker, data-driven, and more accurate property
